💡 To simulate 50% flow at the DCS, inject 8 mA at the transmitter input — that's 25% DP, which the square root extractor converts to 50% flow.
💡 Low-flow cutoff: most flow computers and transmitters have a programmable cutoff around 10% flow (= 1% DP = ~4.16 mA). Below that, output snaps to zero to avoid noisy near-zero readings.
💡 Know where the sqrt happens — in the transmitter (HART config) or in the DCS/flow computer. Injecting mA at the wrong point gives you wrong results. Confirm before you calibrate.
💡 At 25% DP you only have 50% flow. If the operator sees 50% flow and process pressure looks right, the DP cell is likely reading correctly — the relationship is non-linear by design.
