What is an I/P?
Current-to-Pressure converter. Takes a 4–20 mA signal from the DCS/AO card and converts it to a 3–15 PSI pneumatic signal to move a control valve or actuator.
Standard Bench Set
4 mA = 3 PSI (0%)  |  12 mA = 9 PSI (50%)  |  20 mA = 15 PSI (100%). Confirm with the positioner datasheet — some use 6–30 PSI.
Split Range Example
Two valves on one AO: Valve A = 4–12 mA (0–100%), Valve B = 12–20 mA (0–100%). Use the Split Range mode above to calculate each valve's signal range.
No Output / Low Output
Check: instrument air supply (min 20 PSI supply), input mA signal at I/P terminals, zero/span screws on I/P body, clogged nozzle or flapper.
Calibration Steps
1. Apply 4 mA — adjust zero until output = 3 PSI. 2. Apply 20 mA — adjust span until output = 15 PSI. 3. Repeat until stable. Verify at 50% (12 mA = 9 PSI).
Air Supply Requirement
Instrument air must be clean, dry, and at least 5 PSI above max output. For 3–15 PSI output, supply should be minimum 20 PSI.
💡 Tip: If valve hunts or oscillates, check for air supply pressure fluctuation or positioner feedback linkage issues — not the I/P itself.
