🧪 Pressure
How pressure is measured and behaves in the field
The Core Idea

Pressure transmitters measure force per area and convert it into a signal (usually 4–20 mA).

Types of Pressure
🌬️ Gauge Pressure (PSIG)
Measured relative to atmosphere
🌌 Absolute Pressure (PSIA)
Measured from vacuum (0 reference)
⚖️ Differential Pressure (DP)
Difference between two pressures
How it Actually Works
🔧 Process Pressure
Applies force to sensor (diaphragm)
⚙️ Transmitter
Converts pressure into electrical signal
🎛️ Output
Sends 4–20 mA to PLC / DCS
Real Field Behavior
  • Higher pressure → higher mA
  • Stable pressure → stable reading
  • Process changes show directly in signal
Common Problems
❌ Plugged Impulse Line
→ Reading stuck or slow
❌ Leak in Line
→ Reading lower than actual
❌ Bad Calibration
→ Offset or incorrect span
❌ Sensor Damage
→ Unstable or incorrect readings
Quick Troubleshooting
⚠️ Reading not changing
→ Check impulse line for blockage
⚠️ Reading too low
→ Check for leaks or venting
⚠️ Reading offset
→ Check zero calibration
⚠️ Reading unstable
→ Check process conditions or sensor health
Field Tip

Always verify your reference: atmospheric, vacuum, or another pressure. That tells you what you're actually measuring.
