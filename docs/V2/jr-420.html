📈 4–20 mA Basics

This is the foundation of instrumentation. If you understand this, you're ahead of most techs.

What is 4–20 mA?

It’s a signal used to represent a measurement.

  • 4 mA = 0%
  • 20 mA = 100%
Real Field Example

Range: 0–100 psi

  • 4 mA → 0 psi
  • 12 mA → 50 psi
  • 20 mA → 100 psi
Quick Formula

% = (mA - 4) / 16 × 100

Common Mistakes
  • Thinking 0 mA = 0% ❌
  • Forgetting scaling ❌
  • Always verify LRV / URV ✅
Field Tip
