⚙️ CONTROL VALVE

Control Valve (AO)

How a 4–20 mA signal actually moves a valve in the field.

The Fundamentals
What's actually happening?

A control valve does not move from electricity directly. The 4–20 mA signal controls air, and air moves the valve.

🧠 DCS / PLC (AO Card)
Sends a 4–20 mA command signal to the positioner.
🌟 Positioner (I/P Converter)
Converts the mA signal into a proportional air pressure (3–15 psi typical).
💨 Air Supply
Instrument air (typically 20–30 psi) provides the force to stroke the actuator.
⚙️ Actuator / Valve
Air pressure moves the actuator, which opens or closes the valve body.
Valve Action
ATO vs ATC
🟢 Air-To-Open (ATO)
Air pressure opens the valve. Loss of air = valve closes (Fail Closed).
🔴 Air-To-Close (ATC)
Air pressure closes the valve. Loss of air = valve opens (Fail Open).
⚠️ Always confirm ATO vs ATC before stroking. The valve may move opposite of what you expect depending on how the positioner is configured.
Fail States
What happens when things go wrong
💨 Loss of Air
Valve goes to its spring-return fail position (FC or FO depending on design).
🔌 Loss of Signal (4–20 mA)
Positioner may fail to last position, fail closed, or fail open — check the positioner datasheet.
🌟 Positioner Failure
Valve may stop responding, hunt, or drift. Check positioner display/LED if equipped.
Quick Troubleshooting
🔴 Signal changing — valve not moving
→ Check air supply first. No air = no movement, regardless of signal.
🔴 Valve stuck in one position
→ Check positioner feedback linkage. Check for mechanical binding.
🟠 Valve moving backwards (opposite of command)
→ Signal may be reversed at AO card, OR wrong ATO/ATC configuration.
🟠 Weak or slow movement
→ Low air supply pressure, clogged air filter, or weak regulator.
🔴 Valve hunting / unstable
→ Positioner gain too high, or mechanical slop in linkage.
Field Tips
💡 Always check air before touching the signal. 90% of control valve problems start with no air or low air pressure.
💡 Stroke the valve manually at the positioner first. If it moves, the problem is electrical. If it doesn't, the problem is mechanical or pneumatic.
💡 Confirm fail state with the P&ID before removing air or signal — FO vs FC is a safety decision, not a default.
