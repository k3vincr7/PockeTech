⚙️ Control Valve (AO)
How a signal actually moves a valve
What is happening?

A control valve does NOT move from electricity directly. It moves using air, controlled by a 4–20 mA signal.

How it Actually Works
🧠 PLC / DCS (Output)
Sends 4–20 mA signal (command)
🎛️ Positioner (I/P)
Converts signal → air pressure
💨 Air Supply
Provides the force to move actuator
⚙️ Valve / Actuator
Moves based on air pressure
Valve Action (ATO vs ATC)
🔼 Air-To-Open (ATO)
Air pressure OPENS the valve
No air → valve CLOSES (fail closed)
🔽 Air-To-Close (ATC)
Air pressure CLOSES the valve
No air → valve OPENS (fail open)
Fail States (What happens when things go wrong)
❌ Loss of Air
Valve goes to its fail position (open or closed)
❌ Loss of Signal (4–20 mA)
Valve may fail or hold position depending on configuration
❌ Positioner Failure
Valve may stop responding or drift
❌ Air Regulator / Tubing Issue
Weak or no movement even with correct signal
Real Field Example
  • 4 mA → Closed (or Open depending on valve action)
  • 12 mA → 50% Position
  • 20 mA → Fully Open (or Closed depending on setup)
Quick Troubleshooting
⚠️ Signal is changing but valve NOT moving
→ Check air supply FIRST
⚠️ Valve stuck in one position
→ Check positioner or linkage
⚠️ Valve moving backwards
→ Signal reversed OR wrong valve action (ATO vs ATC)
⚠️ Slow or weak movement
→ Check regulator, tubing, or air pressure
Field Tip

No air = no movement. Always check air before touching the signal.
