🧥 LEVEL (DP)

Level Measurement (DP)

How differential pressure tells you what's in the tank.

The Fundamentals
How DP measures level

Level is measured using pressure. The more liquid above a point, the more pressure it creates. A DP transmitter measures the difference between a high-side tap (bottom) and a low-side reference (top).

⬇️ High Side (Bottom Tap)
Sees pressure from the full liquid column above. This is the measurement side.
⬆️ Low Side (Top Tap)
Used as reference — atmosphere for open tanks, vapor space for closed tanks.
⚖️ DP Transmitter
Measures High − Low. The result is proportional to liquid height (hydrostatic head).
Tank Configuration
Open vs Closed Tank
🌫️ Open Tank
Top is open to atmosphere. Low side vents to air. Simple — only liquid height affects the reading.
🔒 Closed / Pressurized
Low side connects to vapor space. Tank pressure cancels out — only liquid level is measured.
⚠️ On a closed tank, the low side must connect to the vapor space — not atmosphere. Otherwise tank pressure adds a constant error to every reading.
Wet Leg vs Dry Leg
What's in the low-side impulse line?
💧 Wet Leg
Low-side impulse line is filled with a reference liquid (constant head pressure). Used when process vapor may condense — keeps the leg from filling unpredictably. Transmitter is calibrated with the wet leg offset accounted for.
🌫️ Dry Leg
Low side stays gas-filled. Simpler setup, but any condensation in the leg will shift your reading. Usually requires periodic draining.
⚠️ If a wet leg loses fluid (leak or evaporation), the transmitter will read high — the head pressure you're subtracting is gone.
Quick Troubleshooting
🔴 Reading stuck — not responding to actual level change
→ Plugged impulse line (high or low side). Check manifold block valves are fully open.
🔴 Reading suddenly shifted high or low
→ Wet leg lost fluid, or condensation entered a dry leg. Check reference leg first.
🟠 Reading drifts slowly over time
→ Slow leg leak, condensation buildup, or process density change. Check leg integrity.
🔴 Reading pegged at 0% or 100%
→ Check 5-valve manifold — equalizer may be open, or one process valve may be closed.
🔴 Reading oscillates or is noisy
→ Turbulence or agitation at the tap. Configure damping at the transmitter.
Field Tips
💡 Always ask: what's on the high side, and what's on the low side? That question tells you the full measurement picture before touching anything.
💡 Before zeroing or calibrating, confirm the 5-valve manifold is in the correct position — equalizer closed, both process valves open.
💡 If you suspect a plugged leg, stroke the drain or vent carefully with pressure equalized. A sudden level shift after clearing confirms the leg was blocked.
