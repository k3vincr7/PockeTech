🫙 Level (DP)
How differential pressure measures level
The Core Idea

Level is measured using pressure. The more liquid above a point, the more pressure it creates.

How it Actually Works
⬇️ High Side (Bottom Tap)
Sees pressure from liquid height
⬆️ Low Side (Top Tap)
Used as reference (atmosphere or vapor space)
⚖️ DP Transmitter
Measures difference between high and low pressure
Open Tank
🌬️ Top is open to atmosphere
Low side = atmosphere
Only liquid height affects measurement
  • More level → more pressure → higher reading
  • Simple setup
Closed Tank
🔒 Tank is pressurized
Low side connected to vapor space
Cancels out tank pressure
  • Measures only liquid level, not tank pressure
Wet Leg vs Dry Leg
💧 Wet Leg
Low side is filled with liquid (constant head pressure)
Used when vapor may condense
🌫️ Dry Leg
Low side stays dry (gas only)
Simpler but sensitive to condensation
Real Field Behavior
  • Higher level → higher DP
  • Wet leg adds constant offset
  • Wrong setup = wrong reading
Common Problems
❌ Plugged impulse line
→ Reading stuck or slow
❌ Wet leg lost fluid
→ Reading shifts or becomes inaccurate
❌ Condensation in dry leg
→ False level reading
❌ Transmitter mounted too low
→ Offset error
Quick Troubleshooting
⚠️ Level not changing
→ Check for plugged lines
⚠️ Reading drifting
→ Check wet leg / condensation
⚠️ Reading too high or low
→ Check reference side setup
Field Tip

Always ask: What is on the high side? What is on the low side? That tells you everything.
