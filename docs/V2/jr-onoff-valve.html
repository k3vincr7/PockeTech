🚦 ON/OFF VALVE

On / Off Valve (DO / XV)

Discrete control using solenoids — how it works and what breaks it.

The Fundamentals
What is an On/Off Valve?

An XV (or block valve) is either fully open or fully closed — it does not modulate like a control valve. The DCS sends a discrete signal (DO) to a solenoid, which routes air to move the actuator.

🧠 DCS / PLC (DO Card)
Sends 24 VDC on/off to energize or de-energize the solenoid.
⚡ Solenoid Valve
Electrically-operated pilot valve that routes instrument air to the actuator.
💨 Air Supply
Instrument air strokes the actuator open or closed.
🚦 Valve / Actuator
Moves to fully open or fully closed position only. No in-between.
Fail States
What happens on power or air loss
FC
Fail Closed — spring closes on loss of power or air
FO
Fail Open — spring opens on loss of power or air
FL
Fail Last — stays in current position (double-acting actuator)
⚠️ Fail state is a safety design decision — always confirm with the P&ID or datasheet before removing air or signal.
Position Feedback (DI)
How the DCS knows where the valve is

Most XVs have limit switches on the actuator that send a Digital Input back to the DCS — one for fully open (ZSO) and one for fully closed (ZSC).

ZSO (Open Limit) = valve confirmed open → DI reads HIGH
ZSC (Closed Limit) = valve confirmed closed → DI reads HIGH
Both DIs LOW = valve in mid-travel or limit switch fault → check mechanically
Both DIs HIGH = wiring fault or limit switch conflict → investigate immediately
Quick Troubleshooting
🔴 DO commanded ON — valve not moving
→ Check DO voltage at field terminal. Then check solenoid coil (should click). Then check air.
🔴 Solenoid clicking but valve doesn't move
→ No air supply, clogged air filter, or actuator mechanical failure.
🟠 Valve moving opposite of command
→ Solenoid wiring reversed, OR fail state is wrong for the expected action.
🟠 DCS shows mid-travel / no feedback
→ Check limit switch adjustment. Check DI wiring back to marshalling.
🔴 Active interlock — DO won't energize
→ Check DCS for active permissives or trip conditions before troubleshooting field equipment.
Field Tips
💡 Always check in order: Power → Solenoid → Air → Valve. Don't skip steps.
💡 You can manually stroke most solenoids by pressing the manual override button on the solenoid body — useful for confirming air is good without a DCS command.
💡 If the DCS shows the valve open but it's physically closed (or vice versa), the limit switch is out of adjustment — not a controls problem.
