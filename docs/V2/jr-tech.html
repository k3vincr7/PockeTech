AI / AO / DI / DO — What & Why

🔌 I/O Types Explained

▼
💡 Golden Rule: Always think from the DCS/PLC perspective. If the signal is coming IN to the DCS = Input. If it's going OUT of the DCS = Output. That's it.
AI
Analog Input
4–20 mA
The DCS is listening. A transmitter in the field is measuring something (pressure, flow, temp, level) and sending a 4–20 mA signal back to the DCS card. The DCS reads it.
📌 FT-1001 flow transmitter → 4–20 mA → AI card
AO
Analog Output
4–20 mA
The DCS is commanding. The DCS sends a 4–20 mA signal out to a control valve positioner or I/P to tell it where to go. The valve listens.
📌 DCS → 4–20 mA → I/P → CV-2001 control valve
DI
Digital Input
24 VDC ON/OFF
The DCS is reading a state. Is that switch open or closed? Is that motor running or stopped? It's a simple yes/no signal coming IN to the DCS — either 24VDC or 0V.
📌 XV-2001 limit switch → OPEN/CLOSED → DI card
DO
Digital Output
24 VDC ON/OFF
The DCS is commanding an action. Energize that solenoid. Start that motor. Trip that valve. The DCS sends 24VDC out to make something happen.
📌 DCS → 24VDC → solenoid → XV-2001 opens

Signal Math

🧮 Quick Conversions

▼
mA → %    (mA − 4) ÷ 16 × 100
% → mA    4 + (% ÷ 100 × 16)
0% = 4 mA  |  25% = 8 mA  |  50% = 12 mA  |  75% = 16 mA  |  100% = 20 mA
3–15 psi (I/P)   3 + (% ÷ 100 × 12)
EU value          LRV + (% ÷ 100 × span)
Square root extraction — use for DP flow only. Linear for pressure, level, temperature.

Common Faults & Fixes

🔥 Troubleshooting

▼
🔴 DCS reads 0% / 4 mA flatline
Cause: No loop power, broken wire, blown fuse, wrong card polarity
Fix: Check 24VDC at TX terminals, verify wiring polarity, check fuse on AI card
🔴 DCS reads 100% / 20 mA flatline
Cause: TX fail-high, shorted wiring, card fault
Fix: Disconnect TX and put mA source on loop — if DCS reads correct, TX is bad
🟠 Noisy / jumping signal
Cause: Shield not grounded, running next to high voltage, loose terminal
Fix: Land shield at one end only (marshaling panel), tighten terminals, re-route cable
🟠 Control valve not responding to DCS
Cause: No air supply, I/P not calibrated, AO card fault, positioner issue
Fix: Check air supply first, verify 4–20 mA at I/P input, bench test positioner
🔴 XV valve not opening on DO command
Cause: Solenoid not energizing, no air, interlock active, wiring fault
Fix: Check DO output voltage at field terminal, verify no active interlocks in DCS
🟠 DCS reading doesn't match field gauge
Cause: Wrong LRV/URV in TX, calibration drift, wrong EU in DCS
Fix: Verify TX range matches DCS range, trim zero and span at TX
🔴 Loop reads negative or below 4 mA
Cause: Reversed polarity on AI card or at TX terminals
Fix: Swap +/− wires at TX or at AI card — check loop drawing for correct polarity
