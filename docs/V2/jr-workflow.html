🔧 FIELD WORKFLOW

Field Workflow

Calibration → Loop Check → Commissioning → Troubleshooting — in order.

1. Calibration
Before you calibrate anything
1
Verify the instrument
Correct tag number, correct range (LRV/URV), correct sensor type. Never calibrate an unverified instrument.
2
Apply a known input
Pressure, mA signal, temperature simulation — whatever matches the measurement type. Your source must be traceable and accurate.
3
Adjust zero and span
Zero = LRV (4 mA). Span = URV (20 mA). Adjust one, re-check the other — they interact.
4
5-point linearity check
Test at 0%, 25%, 50%, 75%, 100%. Output should be linear. Non-linearity means sensor damage or a configuration error.
2. Loop Check
Verifying the full signal path
1
Field → DCS (AI/DI)
Inject a known signal at the field device. Verify the correct value appears in the DCS. Check tag, scaling, and engineering units.
2
DCS → Field (AO/DO)
Command an output from the DCS. Verify the correct voltage or mA arrives at the field device.
3
Check polarity and scaling
4–20 mA must not be reversed. DO logic must match the expected action. Scaling at the AI card must match the transmitter range.
3. Final Element Check
Valves and actuators
⚙️ Control Valve (AO)
Stroke from 0% to 100% via DCS. Verify the valve physically opens and closes in the correct direction. Confirm fail state with P&ID.
🚦 On/Off Valve (XV / DO)
Command open and closed from DCS. Verify ZSO and ZSC limit switch feedback is correct. Confirm fail state with P&ID.
⚠️ Always notify the board operator before stroking any valve in the field. Never stroke without authorization.
4. Troubleshooting Logic
Systematic fault isolation
🔴 Signal good — final element not moving
→ Air problem (control valve) or solenoid/wiring problem (XV). Check field before assuming logic.
🔴 No signal at DCS — no output from field device
→ Follow the path: field device → wiring → marshalling → I/O card. Break it into sections and isolate.
🟠 Wrong reading at DCS
→ Check scaling at AI card vs transmitter range. Check for wiring polarity reversal. Verify transmitter calibration.
🔴 Interlock not responding correctly
→ Check DCS for active permissives and trip conditions. Don't assume hardware before verifying logic.
Field Rules
💡 Always work the signal path in order: Field Device → Wiring → Marshalling → I/O → DCS. Don't jump to the middle — you'll miss the root cause.
💡 Check the simplest thing first: air, power, a blown fuse, a closed block valve. Most problems are mechanical or pneumatic — not logic.
💡 Take readings before you change anything. Document what you found and what you changed. You'll need that information if the fix doesn't hold.
💡 The signal tells you WHAT is happening. The process tells you IF it makes sense. If both agree and the reading is still wrong — check your calibration.
